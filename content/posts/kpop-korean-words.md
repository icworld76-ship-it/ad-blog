# 🎵 Decode Your Favorite K-Pop Songs!
![K-Pop Concert Image](https://images.unsplash.com/photo-1540039155733-5bb30b53aa14?q=80&w=1000)
If you are a K-Pop fan, you've probably noticed certain Korean words showing up in almost every song. Understanding these key cultural words will make listening to K-Pop twice as fun! 

Here are **5 essential Korean words** frequently used in K-Pop lyrics and what they really mean.

---

## 1. 심쿵 (Sim-kung)
* **Meaning:** Heartthrob / Heart-fluttering moment
* **Explanation:** A combination of "Sim-jang" (Heart) and "Kung" (Thump). It describes that sudden moment when your heart skips a beat because someone looks ridiculously attractive or does something charming.

## 2. 대박 (Dae-bak)
* **Meaning:** Awesome / Jackpot / Unbelievable
* **Explanation:** Originally meaning "hitting the jackpot," it's now the go-to word to express shock, admiration, or excitement about an incredible performance or visuals.

## 3. 밀당 (Mil-dang)
* **Meaning:** Push and pull in romantic relationships
* **Explanation:** A slang short for "Mil-go Dang-gi-gi" (Pushing and Pulling). It refers to the subtle flirting game where someone plays hard to get.

## 4. 설렘 (Seol-lem)
* **Meaning:** Fluttering excitement
* **Explanation:** That soft, butterfly-in-the-stomach feeling you get when thinking about someone you like or starting a new journey.

## 5. 추억 (Chu-eok)
* **Meaning:** Precious memories
* **Explanation:** Very common in emotional ballads and nostalgic K-Pop songs. It goes beyond simple "memories"—it implies a deep, cherished emotional recollection.
